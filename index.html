Emily Prentiss, BAU

“It's always sad seeing someone's life reduced to the things they had with them when they died. It's just so clear they didn't know how short their time would be.”

— Emily Prentiss, Criminal Minds, Season 3: In Heat

Emily Prentiss is the a longtime member of the BAU who first joined the team two episodes after Elle Greenaway resigned from the FBI for good. At the end of Season Seven, she resigned from the BAU and accepted a position with Interpol in London. She was replaced by Alex Blake, who held the position until her own departure at the end of Season Nine.

The position was then taken over by Kate Callahan for the duration of Season Ten before her own departure. She rejoined the team in Season Twelve and was then promoted to Unit Chief following the resignation of Aaron Hotchner.

By 2022, Emily has been promoted to Section Chief, a position previously held by the late Erin Strauss and later Mateo Cruz.

1. Background

“So whatever game it is you’re trying to play here, I want no part of it.”

— Emily Prentiss, Criminal Minds, Season 13: Miasma

Emily Prentiss was born on October 12, 1970, at 7:12 a.m. Due to her mother's diplomatic background, Prentiss traveled a lot when she was a child, living in several Middle Eastern countries and at one point in Rome.

She is a graduate of Chesapeake Bay University, where she earned her B.A. in Criminal Justice ("Annihilator") and the University of Yale and has been working for the FBI for a little under ten years, primarily in the Midwest. Due to spending her early life with her parents on diplomatic missions to the Middle East, she speaks fluent Arabic and is familiar with that area's local culture. She also speaks fluent Spanish, French, Italian, and some Russian.

2. Personality

"I think politics makes people distrustful. I think it makes them hate themselves. I think it tears families apart and damages people."

— Emily Prentiss, Criminal Minds, Season 5: The Eyes Have It

Prentiss is very level-headed and calm, sometimes it could be said that she seems to be dealing with the cases in an even more professional manner than some other members of the BAU. However, like Elle before her, Prentiss lets her own emotions get the better of her on a few occasions. She has been shown to react emotionally at the crime scenes and feels a strong surge of empathy for the victims. She also appears highly intelligent and driven. Prentiss is willing to put herself at risk to protect others and has a very sarcastic sense of humor.

3. Evolution

“I've always heard every ending is also a beginning. We just don't know it at the time. I'd like to believe that's true.”

— Emily Prentiss, Criminal Minds, Season 7: Run

As of "Just Getting Started", Prentiss has been promoted to Section Chief, where she wrangles with the ambitious Deputy Director of the FBI for funding and resources while helping her team (now lead by Rossi) investigate a network of serial killers that they believe was formed during the COVID-19 pandemic.

4. Notes

“It never ceases to amaze me how dark obsession can get.”

— Emily Prentiss, Criminal Minds, Season 5: The Performer

  • Prentiss was named after a great friend and fellow coworker of Criminal Minds' advisor, Jim Clemente, who wrote her first episode working full-time with the BAU, "Lessons Learned"
  • Her sidearm was a Glock 19.
  • She speaks Spanish ("Catching Out," "A Rite of Passage," "A Thin Line"), Arabic ("Lessons Learned"), French (Lauren), and Italian ("Demonology") fluently; she is presumed to understand Russian well enough to watch the original 1972 film Solaris with Reid ("Sense Memory"), although her Russian, to her admittance, is "not very good" ("Honor Among Thieves").
  • Her transfer to the BAU was not known to Hotch, and he was caught off guard when she reported for duty.
  • Her first case was in the episode "Lessons Learned", where she helped to interrogate Jamal Abaza behind the scenes.
